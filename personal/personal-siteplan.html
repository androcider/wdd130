Home page

Basic kit /Family kit Have you ever been stuck on the side of the road with a flat tire or other car trouble? How long did it take for help to arrive? Do you have the supplies needed to survive if you are ever in this situation? Here is a basic list of items that you should have in your car to survive the ordeal. Spare tire Jack and wheel wrench Tool kit Flares or safety triangle Blankets Mobile battery pack Drinking water Nonperishable food Flashlight and batteries First aid kit First extinguisher Medications Toilet paper Disinfecting wipes Hand sanitizer Feminine products Ziplock bags Trash bags When making a safety kit for the family additional items need to be added to it to accommodate the additional people. An individual kit for each family member can be made or just 1 large group kit. Some additional item may include: If there is a baby traveling in the car usually the standard diaper bag is sufficient for the trip however make sure to have extra: Formuala/baby food Bottles Diapers Diaper cream Wipes Clothing Once you assemble your supplies, you'll want to store them in sturdy container, like a large vacuum-seal bag or a plastic storage bin. But don't just forget about it. Experts say it's important to check and refresh supplies once or twice a year, so they're in good condition for whatever the road throws your way.

Special Considerations

Special considerations If a pet is part of the family consider adding additional: Pet food Water Feeding and watering bowls Leash Chew toy Winter/summer kit There are some items that need to be added to your kit in order to help survive in extreme climates. Some of those items are: Ice scraper Shovel Sand to aid in tire traction Hand/foot warmers Rain gear Battery powered fan Sunblock Headgear Insect repellent

Other Considerations

Other considerations One of the unseen perils of a survival situation is the mental stress that comes from not knowing when you will be rescued and from the inevitable boredom that comes along with waiting for help. What ever keeps you occupied at home can be useful additions to your safety kit. Examples of this include: Deck of cards Books to read Books to color Crosswords Notepad and pens Knitting Crochet By having something to do while you are waiting for help, you can keep your mind sharp and be able to keep the boredom to a minimum.
