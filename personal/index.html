Have you ever been stuck on the side of the road with a flat tire or other car trouble? How long did it take for help to arrive? Do you have the supplies needed to survive if you are ever in this situation? Here is a basic list of items that you should have in your car to survive the ordeal.

As former USAF Pararescue, I am here to share my knowledge with you to prepare you if you ever find yourself on the side of the road waiting for help or lost.

Basic kit

One of the best ways to prepare is to have a safety kit in your vehicle. Each kit should be tailored to the needs of your family but all have a basic list that should include:

  • Spare tire
  • Jack and wheel wrench
  • Tool kit
  • Flares or safety triangle
  • Blankets
  • Mobile battery pack
  • Drinking water
  • Nonperishable food
  • Flashlight and batteries
  • First aid kit
  • First extinguisher
  • Medications
  • Toilet paper
  • Disinfecting wipes
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Feminine products
  • Ziplock bags
  • Trash bags

Once you assemble your supplies, you'll want to store them in sturdy container, like a large vacuum-seal bag or a plastic storage bin. But don't just forget about it. Experts say it's important to check and refresh supplies once or twice a year, so they're in good condition for whatever the road throws your way.
